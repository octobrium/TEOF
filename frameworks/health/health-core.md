## Why Health Is Layer 2

Health is not a lifestyle choice. It is the substrate upon which all other capacities depend.

Without sufficient energy, higher layers cannot function:
- Cannot think clearly when sleep-deprived
- Cannot build wealth while chronically ill
- Cannot maintain relationships while depressed
- Cannot pursue meaning while body fails

Health is not one priority among many; it is the foundation that makes other priorities possible.

## The Hierarchy of Interventions

Returns follow a power law: few interventions capture most benefit.

### Tier 1: Foundational (Non-Negotiable) — 80% of outcomes

| Intervention | Target |
|--------------|--------|
| Sleep | 7-9 hours, consistent timing |
| Movement | Regular activity, resistance training included |
| Nutrition | Adequate protein, mostly whole foods, appropriate calories |
| Avoidance | No smoking, limited alcohol |

### Tier 2: Significant (High Return)

- Sleep optimization (light exposure, temperature, timing)
- Structured exercise (progressive overload, zone 2 cardio)
- Protein optimization (1g/lb, distributed across meals)
- Metabolic markers (fasting glucose, triglycerides, body composition)
- Social connection and stress management

### Tier 3: Marginal (Optimization)

- Supplementation (most offer little)
- Meal timing
- Advanced training periodization
- Biometric tracking
- Environmental optimization

### Tier 4: Speculative (Uncertain Evidence)

- Longevity drugs (rapamycin, metformin for non-diabetics)
- Extreme dietary protocols
- Biohacking interventions
- Unproven supplements

## The Minimum Viable System

### Sleep (Foundation)

- Fixed wake time (same every day, including weekends)
- 8 hours sleep opportunity protected
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- Dark, cool bedroom
- No caffeine after noon

### Nutrition (80/20)

- Protein target: 1g per pound body weight
- Each meal: Protein + vegetables
- Minimal ultra-processed foods
- No liquid calories (except protein shakes if needed)

### Exercise (Minimum Effective Dose)

- Resistance training: 2x/week (can grow to 3-4x)
- Zone 2 cardio: 150 min/week (3x50 min or 5x30 min)
- Daily movement: 7,000+ steps

### Mental Health (Daily Maintenance)

- Morning: Light exposure, no phone for 30 min
- Throughout day: Scheduled breaks
- Evening: Wind-down routine
- Weekly: Nature exposure, social connection

## The Implementation Hierarchy

```
[1] DECISION → [2] ENVIRONMENT → [3] SYSTEMS → [4] HABITS → [5] IDENTITY
```

1. **Decision** — Genuine commitment with specific reason
2. **Environment** — Design for success (reduce friction for good, increase for bad)
3. **Systems** — Schedules, routines, triggers, tracking, accountability
4. **Habits** — Behaviors requiring no decision (cue → routine → reward)
5. **Identity** — "I'm someone who trains" not "I'm trying to exercise"

**Key insight:** Environment design is one-time investment with continuous returns. Willpower is depletable; environment is not.

## Daily Architecture

### Morning
- No phone 30-60 min
- Hydrate (16-20 oz water)
- Light exposure (outside if possible)
- Brief movement
- Protein-forward breakfast (30-40g)

### Mid-Day
- Protein + vegetables lunch
- Walk after eating (blood sugar)
- Movement break every 60-90 min

### Evening
- Workout (if training day)
- Protein + vegetables dinner
- Dim lights 1-2 hours before bed
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- Relaxation activities

### Night
- Fixed bedtime
- Cool, dark room
- 7-9 hours sleep opportunity

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Diagnosis | Solution |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| "No time" | Priority problem, not scarcity | Time audit; combine activities; morning workouts |
| "No results" | Consistency/patience | Track honestly for 2 weeks; physiological change takes months |
| "Keep falling off" | Scope/environment | Never miss twice; minimum viable version; environment check |

## Phases

1. **Foundation (Weeks 1-4):** Consistency over intensity. Fixed sleep, basic protein, 2x training, daily walking.
2. **Development (Weeks 5-12):** Progressive improvement. Training progression, nutrition refinement, adding cardio.
3. **Optimization (Month 4+):** Fine-tuning with data. Biomarkers, advanced programming, marginal gains.
