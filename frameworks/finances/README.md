## CORE PRINCIPLES (from sources)

### From Value Investing (Graham/Buffett)
- Distinguish price from value
- Margin of safety in all positions
- Long-term holding; let compounding work
- Stay within circle of competence
- Mr. Market is manic-depressive—ignore daily noise

### From Macro Analysis (Dalio, etc.)
- Diversification across uncorrelated assets
- Position for multiple scenarios, not predictions
- Understand monetary cycles and their effects

### From Monetary Economics
- Money is created through lending, not printing
- Inflation is dilution of monetary claims
- Those closest to money creation benefit first (Cantillon effect)
- Fiat currencies require ongoing maintenance of collective belief

## KEY POSITIONS

The framework argues for:

1. **Understanding > Following advice** — Know how money works, don't just follow tips
2. **Assets > Currency** — Hold assets that can't be diluted by money creation
3. **Diversification > Concentration** — Position for multiple scenarios
4. **Long-term > Short-term** — Compound returns over decades
5. **Income generation > Speculation** — Build income streams, not trading profits

## METHODOLOGICAL COMMITMENTS

From source frameworks + TEOF integration:

1. **Empirical over narrative** — What has actually worked historically
2. **Mechanisms over predictions** — How money works, not what markets will do
3. **Positioning over timing** — Survive all scenarios, don't predict one
4. **Long-term over short-term** — Decades, not quarters
5. **Understanding over rules** — Know why, so you can adapt
