# Scientific Method Analysis

**Domain:** Knowledge acquisition, truth-seeking
**Track Record:** ~400 years (formalized), roots in ancient empiricism
**Observable Outcomes:** Cumulative technological progress, predictive accuracy, replicable results

---

## Why It Persists

The scientific method is the only knowledge system that:
1. **Produces cumulative progress** - Each generation builds on the last
2. **Self-corrects** - Wrong theories eventually get falsified
3. **Generates predictive power** - Theories predict outcomes that can be tested
4. **Scales across cultures** - Works regardless of who applies it

Other knowledge systems (tradition, revelation, authority) can preserve knowledge but don't reliably generate new, verifiable knowledge.

---

## Core vs Peripheral

**Core (stable, rarely changes):**
- Empiricism: Claims must be testable against reality
- Falsifiability: Good theories can be proven wrong
- Replication: Results must be reproducible by others
- Peer review: Claims checked by independent observers
- Hypothesis → Test → Revise loop

**Peripheral (adapts to context):**
- Specific statistical methods (evolve over time)
- Publication formats (journals → preprints → etc.)
- Funding structures (varies by era/country)
- Instrumentation (constantly improving)
- Disciplinary norms (differ between physics, biology, etc.)

---

## Selection Pressures Survived

1. **Religious opposition** - Galileo, Darwin, etc. Science persisted despite institutional resistance
2. **Political capture** - Lysenkoism, Nazi physics. Bad science eventually falsified.
3. **Fraud** - Individual fraudsters caught by replication failures
4. **Paradigm shifts** - Newton → Einstein. Framework adapted, core method remained.
5. **Funding pressures** - "Publish or perish" distorts but hasn't killed core method

---

## Observable Outcomes

**Practitioners demonstrate:**
- Technology that works (planes fly, vaccines protect, phones connect)
- Predictions that land (eclipses, chemical reactions, disease spread)
- Cross-cultural agreement (physics works the same in every country)
- Cumulative knowledge (each generation knows more than the last)

**Non-practitioners lack:**
- Reliable method for distinguishing true from false claims
- Cumulative progress (traditions cycle, don't accumulate)
- Predictive power for novel situations

## Integration Notes

**Aligns with:**
- TEOF observation primacy (empiricism = observation first)
- TEOF "contradictions collapse" (falsification)
- TEOF recursive refinement (hypothesis → test → revise)

**Conflicts with:**
- Religious frameworks on specific factual claims (age of earth, evolution)
  - Resolution: Use science for empirical claims, religion for meaning/practice
- Traditional authority ("we've always done it this way")
  - Resolution: Tradition as hypothesis, test if outcomes still hold

**Does NOT address:**
- Meaning, purpose, ethics (science describes, doesn't prescribe)
- Personal discipline, community building
- What *should* be done (only what *is* the case)

---

## What to Borrow

1. **Falsifiability mindset** - Ask "what would prove me wrong?" for all beliefs
2. **Hypothesis → Test → Revise loop** - Apply to personal decisions, not just formal research
3. **Replication principle** - If it only worked once, be suspicious
4. **Empiricism** - Reality trumps theory, always
5. **Cumulative approach** - Build on what's known, don't reinvent

---

## What to Leave

1. **Formal publication** - Overkill for personal knowledge
2. **Statistical rigor requirements** - Useful for research, impractical for daily decisions
3. **Peer review bureaucracy** - The principle matters, not the institutional form
4. **Domain-specific norms** - Physics conventions don't apply to life decisions

---

## TEOF Connection

The scientific method is Pattern C applied to knowledge:
- **Core:** Empiricism, falsifiability, replication (stable across 400 years)
- **Operational:** Peer review, hypothesis testing, publication (adapts per era)
- **Tactical:** Specific experiments, statistical methods (changes constantly)

TEOF doesn't replace science. TEOF recognizes science as the reality-tested framework for knowledge acquisition and borrows its core principles.
