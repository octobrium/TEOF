# Chapter 9b: Asset Ranking for Current Conditions

---

## 9b.1 The Ranking Framework

Rather than presenting subjective preferences as analysis, this section attempts to rank assets using explicit criteria with transparent reasoning. You may weight these criteria differently based on your situation.

**Criteria used for ranking:**

1. **Inflation protection** - Does the asset preserve purchasing power during monetary expansion?
2. **Liquidity** - Can you exit the position when needed without major loss?
3. **Counterparty risk** - Does value depend on another party's solvency or honesty?
4. **Seizure resistance** - Can the asset be confiscated or frozen by authorities?
5. **Historical performance** - What does the empirical track record show?
6. **Current valuation** - Is the asset cheap or expensive relative to history?
7. **Tail risk protection** - Does it hold value in crisis scenarios?
8. **Accessibility** - Can ordinary investors easily obtain it?

Each asset is scored 1-5 on each criterion, with rationale provided.

## 9b.4 Important Qualifications

### This Ranking Is Time-Specific

Current conditions (late 2024/2025):
- Elevated equity valuations
- Interest rates above recent history but below long-term average
- Fiscal trajectory unsustainable
- Monetary expansion paused but not reversed
- Geopolitical risk elevated
- AI narrative driving tech valuations

Different conditions would produce different rankings.

### Your Situation Matters

- **Age/time horizon:** Young investors can tolerate volatility for higher expected returns
- **Liquidity needs:** Near-term needs require liquid assets regardless of return
- **Tax situation:** Different assets have different tax treatment
- **Risk tolerance:** Bitcoin's volatility is intolerable for some
- **Skill/interest:** Real estate rewards active management; index funds don't
- **Jurisdiction:** Some assets are more accessible or favorable in different countries

### Concentration Risk

Any single asset can fail:
- Bitcoin could be banned or broken
- Equities could enter decade-long bear market (Japan 1989-2020)
- Gold could be confiscated (US 1933)
- Real estate markets can crash (2008)
- Cash loses to inflation certainly

Diversification across ranking is more robust than concentration in the top-ranked asset.

### The Ranking Is Debatable

Different people with different assumptions would produce different rankings. This is not objective truth but one analytical framework. Apply your own judgment.

## 9b.5 Suggested Allocation by Profile

### Young, High Risk Tolerance, Long Horizon

- Bitcoin: 25-40%
- US Equities: 30-40%
- Gold: 5-10%
- Short-term Treasuries/Cash: 10-20%
- Real Estate: When capital allows

**Rationale:** Maximize expected return; volatility tolerance allows riding drawdowns.

### Mid-Career, Moderate Risk, Growing Obligations

- Bitcoin: 10-20%
- US Equities: 40-50%
- Gold: 10-15%
- Short-term Treasuries/Cash: 15-25%
- Real Estate: Primary residence + potentially rental

**Rationale:** Balance growth with stability; obligations require reliability.

### Near Retirement, Lower Risk, Preservation Focus

- Bitcoin: 5-10%
- US Equities: 30-40%
- Gold: 15-20%
- Short-term Treasuries/Cash: 25-35%
- Real Estate: Primary residence

**Rationale:** Preservation matters more than growth; volatility tolerance lower.

### High Net Worth, Maximum Optionality

- Bitcoin: 10-20%
- US Equities: 25-35%
- Gold/Precious Metals: 10-15%
- Real Estate: 20-30% (multiple properties, potentially multiple jurisdictions)
- Private Equity/Alternatives: 10-20%
- Cash/Short-term: 10-15%

**Rationale:** Diversification across asset classes, geographies, and regimes.

## 9b.7 The Meta-Point

No one knows the future. Every allocation is a bet on scenarios that may not occur.

The goal is not to find the "right" allocation but to find one that:
- You can actually hold through volatility
- Covers multiple scenarios without being optimal for none
- Matches your actual constraints and timeline
- You understand well enough to maintain conviction

An adequate allocation you can hold beats an optimal allocation you abandon during drawdowns.

---

*Assets are not equally valuable in all conditions. Understanding why each is ranked as it is—and what would change the ranking—enables informed adaptation as conditions evolve.*
