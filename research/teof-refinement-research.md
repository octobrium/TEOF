## What the Research Says Actually Works

### 1. Implementation Intentions (If-Then Planning)

**Effect size: d = 0.65 (medium-to-large)**

The most replicated finding in behavioral psychology. From [Gollwitzer & Sheeran meta-analysis](https://cancercontrol.cancer.gov/sites/default/files/2020-06/goal_intent_attain.pdf) of 94 studies.

**How it works:**
- Specify: "If [situation X], then I will [behavior Y]"
- Creates automatic triggering (bypasses conscious decision)
- Shifts from top-down to bottom-up processing

**Key moderators (from [642-study meta-analysis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10463283.2024.2334563)):**
- Effect larger when if-then format is explicit
- Effect larger when plans are rehearsed at least once
- Effect larger when motivation is high
- Personalized plans > prompted plans

**TEOF Application:**
```
Current: "I should exercise" (goal intention)
Better: "When I wake up, before checking phone, I will do 10 pushups" (implementation intention)
```

---

### 2. Commitment Devices / Precommitment

**Theoretical foundation: [Ulysses contracts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commitment_device)**

From behavioral economics: humans are "imperfectly rational" — capable of planning but prone to deviate due to weakness of will.

**What works:**
- External constraints that make defection costly
- Public commitment (reputational stakes)
- Financial stakes (loss aversion)
- Environmental design (remove choice)

**Evidence:**
- [stickK.com](https://www.stickk.com) studies show commitment contracts improve goal completion
- Hotel study: 25% greater towel reuse with public commitment + visible signal
- Greater cost of breaking = greater effectiveness ([Dolan et al., 2010](https://www.behavioraleconomics.com/resources/mini-encyclopedia-of-be/commitment/))

**TEOF Application:**
- Make commitments public (accountability partner, social media)
- Create stakes (money, reputation)
- Design environment to reduce friction for desired behavior
- Remove options for undesired behavior (delete apps, not just willpower)

---

### 3. Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

**The three psychological needs:** Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness

From [40+ years of research](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/theory/):

| Need | Definition | When Satisfied |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Autonomy | Feeling of choice/ownership | Self-selected goals |
| Competence | Sense of mastery/effectance | Appropriate challenge |
| Relatedness | Connection to others | Social support/belonging |

**Key insight:** Quality of motivation matters more than quantity.
- **Autonomous motivation** (internal, chosen) → sustained behavior
- **Controlled motivation** (external pressure) → compliance only

**TEOF Application:**
- Goals must be self-chosen (not imposed by AI or system)
- Challenge level must match current capacity
- Connection to community/accountability matters
- Rationale for behaviors increases adherence

---

### 4. Habit Formation Science

**Key findings from [Nature Communications study](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42003-023-04500-2):**

- Average time to automaticity: ~66 days (not 21)
- Considerable individual variation
- Context stability crucial

**The Habit Loop (from [PMC research](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3505409/)):**
```
Cue → Craving → Response → Reward
```

**What facilitates habit formation:**
- Repetition in stable context
- Immediate reinforcement
- Self-selected behaviors (autonomy)
- Starting small (reduce friction)

**What breaks habits:**
- Avoiding habit cues
- Forming competing associations
- Environmental redesign
- Goal-directed inhibition

**TEOF Application:**
- Anchor new behaviors to existing cues
- Same time, same place, same preceding action
- Immediate reward after behavior (not delayed)
- Start with minimum viable version

---

### 5. Metacognition and Self-Regulation

**Effect size: +7-8 months learning progress** (from [Education Endowment Foundation](https://educationendowmentfoundation.org.uk/education-evidence/teaching-learning-toolkit/metacognition-and-self-regulation))

**What works:**
- Explicit teaching of planning, monitoring, evaluating
- Modeling thinking aloud
- Combining cognitive + metacognitive strategies
- Appropriate challenge level

**Key finding:** Disadvantaged individuals less likely to use these strategies unless explicitly taught.

**TEOF Application:**
- TEOF already does this (Observe → Recurse → Refine → Realign)
- Gap is in explicit prompting during action, not just reflection
- Need monitoring during behavior, not just post-hoc analysis

---

### 6. What Doesn't Work (or Is Contested)

**Ego Depletion / Decision Fatigue:**
- [Replication crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ego_depletion) — 2016 multi-lab study failed to replicate
- Effect may be belief-dependent (cultural variation)
- "Willpower as muscle" model not supported

**Implication:** Don't design around "conserving willpower." Focus on implementation intentions, environment design, and habit formation instead.

**Generic goal-setting without implementation specifics:**
- "I want to be healthier" → weak
- Goals without if-then plans → [intention-behavior gap](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0065260106380021) remains

## Proposed TEOF Refinements

### 1. Implementation Intention Protocol

Add to daily practice:

```markdown
## Today's Implementation Intentions

| Goal | If... | Then... |
|------|-------|---------|
| Exercise | I wake up | I do 10 pushups before phone |
| Diet | I feel urge to snack | I drink water and wait 10 min |
| Focus | I open laptop | I write for 25 min before checking anything |
```

**Why:** d = 0.65 effect size. Most replicated behavioral intervention.

---

### 2. Commitment Architecture

For each non-negotiable:

| Anchor | Commitment Device | Stakes |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Exercise | Public log (Twitter/friend) | Reputation |
| Sleep | Phone in other room at 10pm | Environmental |
| One step toward goal | Tell someone what you'll do | Social |

**Why:** Precommitment bypasses in-the-moment weakness of will.

---

### 3. Cue-Routine-Reward Mapping

For each desired habit:

```markdown
## Habit: Morning Exercise

**Cue:** Feet hit floor
**Routine:** 10 pushups (minimum viable)
**Reward:** Coffee only after completion

**Context stability:**
- Same location (bedroom floor)
- Same time (immediately on waking)
- Same preceding action (alarm off)
```

**Why:** Habit formation requires stable context + immediate reinforcement.

---

### 4. Real-Time Metacognitive Prompts

Not just end-of-day reflection. During the day:

- Before starting task: "What's my intention here?"
- When distracted: "What was I doing? Does this serve the goal?"
- When resisting: "What's the implementation intention for this situation?"

**Why:** Metacognition during action > post-hoc reflection.

---

### 5. Autonomy Preservation

All of the above must be:
- Self-selected (not AI-imposed)
- Rationale understood (not just "because TEOF says")
- Appropriately challenging (not overwhelming)

**Why:** Controlled motivation → compliance only. Autonomous motivation → sustained change.

## Addendum: Corrected Conclusion (2024-11-29)

### What We Tried

Created "tactical" commandment-style distillations of each framework — condensed if-then rules meant to serve as a "praxis layer."

### Why It Failed

Further research revealed:

1. **Condensed rules are cargo cult.** The Ten Commandments format works via divine authority, community enforcement, ritual reinforcement, and lifetime exposure — not the format itself. [Replication studies](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2018-06-replication-outcome-famous-ten.html) showed the "moral reminder" effect was close to zero.

2. **Simplification strips context.** When AI reads full frameworks, outputs reflect your thinking. When AI reads condensed rules, outputs are generic. The long-form docs ARE the operating context.

3. **The research supports practices, not documents:**
   - Implementation intentions must be *personalized* and *rehearsed* — not generic lists
   - Commitment devices require *real stakes* — not written promises
   - Relatedness requires *actual community* — not imagined support
   - Metacognition requires *prompts during action* — not reading about it

### The Actual System

The praxis layer is not a document. It's the operating loop:

```
Long-form docs (your externalized thinking)
        │
        │ AI reads fully
        ▼
AI generates output (grounded in your context)
        │
        │ You verify
        ▼
Human Gate (accurate? useful? aligned?)
        │
        ├── Good → Action / refine docs
        └── Bad → Discard, correct
```

This loop:
- Minimizes hallucination (grounded in verified docs)
- Preserves your thinking (full context, not stripped)
- Catches errors (Human Gate)
- Improves over time (periodic refinement)

### What's Actually Missing (Practices, Not Documents)

|-----|----------|-------------|
| Implementation intentions | Create personalized if-thens | You |
| Commitment devices | Set up real stakes/accountability | You |
| Relatedness/community | Find actual partners | You |
| Real-time metacognition | Practice during action | You |

None of these are solved by adding more text to TEOF.
