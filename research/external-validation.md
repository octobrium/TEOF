## 3. Paradox Dissolution Method

**TEOF Claim (from TEOF-complete.md:904-926):**
1. Recognize structural undecidability
2. Redirect to action and functional outcomes
3. Performative test exposes self-contradiction
4. Functional resolution through orientation

### External Validation

| TEOF Element | External Source | Finding |
|--------------|-----------------|---------|
| Redirect undecidable questions to practical outcomes | [William James, Pragmatism (1907)](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/pragmatism/) | "What difference would it practically make?" - disputes with no practical difference are "idle" |
| Dissolve rather than solve paradoxes | [Wittgenstein, Philosophical Investigations (1953)](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/wittgenstein/) | "Show the fly the way out of the fly-bottle"; problems "dissolved like a lump of sugar in water" |
| Performative contradiction as test | [Habermas/Apel, Cambridge Lexicon](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/cambridge-habermas-lexicon/performative-selfcontradiction/E6687FBEE3B506BB5FE184E6D6250E88) | Denying rationality while arguing is self-refuting |
| "Observation cannot be denied" structure | [Hintikka (1962)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Performative_contradiction) | Descartes' cogito as performative structure |

### Philosophical Lineage

TEOF's paradox-dissolution method stands in established philosophical tradition:

1. **Descartes (1637)** - Cogito establishes self-evidence through performative structure
2. **James (1907)** - Pragmatism: undecidable metaphysical disputes dissolve when practical consequences are identical
3. **Wittgenstein (1953)** - Philosophical problems are language confusions to be dissolved, not solved
4. **Habermas/Apel (1970s-80s)** - Performative contradiction as formal test for self-refuting claims

**Verdict:** **Externally validated** - TEOF synthesizes established methods (pragmatism, therapeutic philosophy, performative contradiction).
