## The Alignment Property

This goal structure is self-correcting.

**The problem it solves:** Any goal taken to extremes by a capable optimizer produces catastrophe (paperclip problem).

**Why "unify observation" doesn't:**
- Observation requires difference (nothing to observe without distinction)
- Unity requires multiplicity (can't unify one thing)
- The optimizer is *inside* observation, not outside
- Catastrophic paths generate observable effects
- Those effects must be unified — suppressing them violates the goal

An optimizer pursuing "unify observation" cannot ignore its own effects without contradicting the goal. The goal includes the goal-pursuer in its scope.

**Test:** Present this frame to any capable reasoner. Self-correcting conclusion emerges without external intervention.
