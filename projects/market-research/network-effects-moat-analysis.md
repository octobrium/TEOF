## TEOF's Design Argues Against Forking

**Per L1 (Unity):** Forking fragments identity. Unified TEOF > 50 variants.

**Per L3 (Propagation):** Framework propagates better as unified standard.

**Per L4 (Defense):** Fragmentation weakens; unification strengthens.

**Per L6 (Truth):** More observers verifying = higher confidence. Solo fork loses collective verification.

**Per L7 (Power):** Coordination requires shared protocol. Forks can't coordinate with each other.

**Insight:** Users who understand TEOF deeply will recognize that *staying unified is more coherent than forking*.
